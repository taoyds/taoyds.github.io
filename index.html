Bio

Tao Yu is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at The University of Hong Kong and a director of the XLANG Lab (as part of the HKU NLP Group). He spent one year in the UW NLP Group working with Noah Smith, Luke Zettlemoyer, and Mari Ostendorf. He completed his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Yale University, advised by Dragomir Radev and master's at Columbia University advised by Owen Rambow and Kathleen McKeown.

Tao has received the Google and Amazon faculty research awards (Google Research Scholar Award 2023, Amazon Research Award 2022). His main research interest is in Natural Language Processing. His research aims to build language model agents that transform (“grounding”) language instructions into code or actions executable in real-world environments, including databases, web applications, and the physical world etc,. It lies at the heart of language model agents or natural language interfaces that can interact with and learn from these real-world environments to facilitate human interaction with data analysis, web applications, and robotic instruction through conversation. It involves:

  • Executable Language Grounding: Semantic Parsing and Code Generation (Binder (ICLR 2023), DS-1000 (ICML 2023), Spider (EMNLP 2018), Coder-Reviewer Reranking (ICML 2023), Test Suite Eval (EMNLP 2020)), Structured Knowledge Grounding (UnifiedSKG (EMNLP 2022))
  • Efficient and Generalizable Large Language Models: Instructor (ACL Findings 2023), Selective Annotation (ICLR 2023), ZeroGen (EMNLP 2022), GraPPa (ICLR 2021)
  • Dialog and Interactive Systems: ICL-DST (EMNLP Findings 2022), SParC (ACL 2019), CoSQL (EMNLP 2019), NL2Interface (NLVIZ 2022)

We are actively looking for strong and motivated students to join our group! If you are interested in working with us, please read recent papers, fill in the form with thoughts on extensions. Sorry, I'm afraid I generally can't respond to all individual emails.
